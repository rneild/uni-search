## Notes

Research date: 2026-03-23. Sources: Skidmore official site (skidmore.edu), US News, HPAC statistics page, Niche, Princeton Review, WeatherSpark.

### Premed Track — HPAC

Skidmore's Health Professions Advisory Committee is a multi-faculty body with genuine institutional commitment to pre-health outcomes. The 81% medical school acceptance rate (vs. 41% national average) is a statistic Skidmore publishes directly from its own HPAC data — it is not a marketing claim but a tracked outcome. Advising includes committee letters, mock interviews, MCAT preparation guidance, and a specialist career advisor (Rachel Chase) dedicated to health and science careers. For a student whose primary goal is forensic pathology via an MD, this infrastructure matters.

### No Forensic Science Curriculum

The only forensic-adjacent course is a Scribner Seminar (freshman experience) called "Forensic Science and Criminal (In)Justice" — a single seminar covering philosophy of science, forensic analysis, and wrongful conviction case studies. There is no major, minor, or course sequence in forensic science or criminology. This is a real gap if she wanted coursework in the subject. However, the forensic pathology pathway runs through premed → medical school → pathology residency → forensic pathology fellowship, not through an undergraduate forensic science degree. The absence is less critical than it might appear.

### Neuroscience as an Emerging Option

Skidmore has a full standalone neuroscience major with its own research infrastructure. Given her interest in forensic pathology, psychology, and how the human body works, neuroscience is a plausible major or minor that hasn't come up in the shortlist discussion yet. Worth flagging to her as an option that bridges biology, psychology, and medicine.

### Financial Aid Commitment

Skidmore meets 100% of demonstrated need for admitted students. This is a meaningful commitment — though not as strong as need-blind schools like the Ivies or Amherst, it does mean that if the family qualifies for need-based aid, Skidmore will fully cover the gap between COA and Expected Family Contribution. The average net price for aid recipients (~$32–34k) is comparable to Union's (~$34k). The Princeton Review #1 ranking for financial aid reflects this commitment. The unknowns are (a) whether the family has demonstrated need, and (b) what merit awards are available to reduce cost further.

### ED Strategy Note

With 54% of each class admitted via binding Early Decision, the Regular Decision pool is meaningfully more competitive. If Skidmore is genuinely her top choice at application time, ED is the strategically correct move. This should be factored into the application plan once the shortlist is narrowed.
